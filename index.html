THE PROBLEM
My school is trying to determine the amount of students that like chocolate ice cream to see whether or not they should add chocolate ice cream to the CCA (College and Career Academy) lunch lines. (Unfortunately, my school is not actually planning on adding chocolate ice cream to the lunch lines; this was just a made-up scenario for my group project in AP Statistics)
CONDITIONS
RANDOMNESS
In order to construct a confidence interval, we must make sure our sample if random. To do this, we went around during lunch and used tables as clusters and randomly selected tables to collect opinions from. The advantage of collecting our sample from the lunches is that they are representative of the whole school, containing all grade levels.
INDEPENDENCE
Since we are sampling without replacement, we need to ensure that the sample fulfills the 10% rule of being less that 10% of the population. Since 56 is less than 10% of all students at the school, the sample is independent.
$$\huge 56 \lt 10 \% \text{ of all students}$$
NORMALITY
We need to ensure that our sample is normally distributed. We can use the Large Counts Condition to check for normality by multiplying our sample size (56) by our p-hat (0.5) to see if it’s greater than or equal to 10. NOTE: We also need to check (1 – p-hat), but (1 – 0.5) is still 0.5.
$$\Large 56 \ \bullet \ 0.5 \ge 10$$
$$\Large 56 \ \bullet \ (1-0.5) \ge 10$$
CONCLUSION
CALCULATION
$$\Large 0.5 \ \pm \ 1.96 \sqrt{\frac{0.5 (1-0.5)}{56}}=(0.369, 0.631)$$
CONCLUSION
We are 95% confident that the true value of proportion of students who like chocolate ice cream is between (0.369, 0.631).
